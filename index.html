Coffy On Coffee

Published on Febuary, 23 2023

Photo of Coffy Drinking Matcha

Caffeine is a widely used psychoactive substance, and it is pretty common for teenagers to consume large amounts of caffeine daily due to fatigue. However, there are numerous concerns about the effects of caffeine on teenagers, given that their bodies and brains are still developing. In this article, we will explore the effect that caffeine can have on teens as well as the recommended limits for caffeine intake.

Firstly, it is important that one understands that caffeine is a stimulant that affects the central nervous system. Caffeine is found in many drinks and foods such as coffee, tea, chocolate, and energy drinks. When consumed, caffeine blocks the action of a neurotransmitter called adenosine which usually causes drowsiness and slows down brain activity. This blockage will result in an increase in awareness, improved mood, and decreased fatigue.

When consumed in large amounts, caffeine can lead to several adverse effects, more prominent in teenagers. For instance, caffeine can interfere with sleep, leading to insomnia and sleep deprivation, which can negatively impact a teenager's health, mentally and physically. This insomnia can also lead to a need for more caffeine to cure that exhaustion which in turn prevents sleep, causing a loop that is increasingly hard to break out of. Caffeine can also cause an increase in nervousness and anxiety, leading to decreased academic and social performance.

Given these issues, the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that teenagers shouldnâ€™t consume more than 100mg of caffeine per day. This limit is significantly lower than the safe limit for adults, which is 400-500mg per day. It is also worth noting that the amount of caffeine in a drink of food varies depending on the type and brand.

So, what can be done with this information? If you're a teenager who consumes caffeine regularly, it's essential to monitor your intake and ensure that you don't exceed the recommended limit. You can do this by keeping track of the amount of caffeine you consume daily and avoiding drinks and foods with high caffeine content, such as energy drinks. Additionally, you can consider switching to caffeine-free alternatives or gradually reducing your caffeine intake to avoid withdrawal symptoms.
